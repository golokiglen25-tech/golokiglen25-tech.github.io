This is where the house truly begins! Getting the foundations right is crucial for an elevated home, ensuring stability and a level structure for years to come.

Detailed Steps for Week 1:

  1. Site Clearing & Layout:
    • Clear the 10m x 7m area of vegetation and debris.
    • Mark out the exact footprint with pegs and string, ensuring perfect 90-degree corners using the 3-4-5 rule.
  2. Digging Post Holes:
    • Dig holes for your main support posts (typically every 2.5m - 3.5m).
    • Ensure each hole is at least 600mm to 900mm deep, depending on soil stability.
  3. Post Preparation & Placement:
    • Treat the bottom 1 meter of each timber post with tar or old engine oil to prevent rot and termite damage.
    • Place a flat rock or small concrete pad at the bottom of each hole as a "sole plate" before inserting the posts.
  4. Alignment & Leveling:
    • Ensure all 15 posts are perfectly plumb and aligned with the string lines.
